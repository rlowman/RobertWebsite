The Story of Robert Lowman III

On October 15th, 1995 I was born in Chestertown, Maryland to my parents Dale Lowman and Karen Lowman (Zimmerman). Growing up, I excelled in the subjects math and science. Alongside with focusing on my schoolwork I played both lacrosse and football and loved being a student athlete.

After graduating from Kent County High School, I decided to take my talents both on the lacrosse field and in the classroom to King's College in Wilkes-Barre Pennslyvania. All four years I attended King's I was a member of the varsity lacrosse team, as well as being a team captain my Junior and Senior year. Originally being a Computer Information Systems major, I was required to take an introduction to programming course. Even though this course was the first time I had ever seen code, I was very successful in this class and realized my true potential as a coder. After speaking with my progfessor, I decided to change majors to Computer Science to focus on coding and development, and have enjoyed the field ever since.

When I am not writing code or researching topics in the field, you can find me on the ski mountain with my friends. If it is during summer and there is no snow on the ground, I love to go out on the Chesapeake Bay and fish or wakeboard. Other hobbies of mine include attending EDM and rap concerts, and traveling with my friends.

In 5-10 years, I can vision myself being a full-stack developer who is very experienced in the field. While I plan to live in the Philadelphia area for a couple of years, I would love to move out west to the mountains and be able to ski on a larger scale more often.
